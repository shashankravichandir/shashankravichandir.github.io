Resources

A non-exhaustive list of engaging and illustrative books and courses on various topics in math and physics that I have greatly benefitted from as well as a list of books on miscellaneous topics that have influenced me deeply.

Math

  • Introduction to Tensor Analysis and Calculus of Surfaces by Pavel Grinfeld
  • Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos by Steven Strogatz - Book here
  • Asymptotics and Perturbation Methods by Steven Strogatz
  • Complex Variables: Introduction and Applications by Mark Ablowitz and Athanassios Fokas
  • Advanced Mathematical Methods for Scientists and Engineers by Carl Bender and Steven Orszag
  • Handbook of Stochastic Methods by Crispin Gardiner

Physics

  • V Balakrishnan's lectures on Classical Physics, Quantum Physics, Non-equilibrium Statistical Physics, and Selected Topics in Mathematical Physics.
  • Fundamentals of Statistical and Thermal Physics by Frederick Rief
  • David Tong's Lecture notes on Theoretical Physics - Statistical Field Theory
  • An Introduction to Fluid Dynamics by G K Batchelor

Miscellaneous

  • Phantoms in the Brain: Probing the Mysteries of the Human Mind by V S Ramachandran and Sandra Blakeslee
  • Fermat's Enigma by Simon Singh
  • What is Life by Erwin Schrodinger
  • The Selfish Gene by Richard Dawkins
  • Guns, Germs, and Steel by Jared Diamond
  • Thinking Fast and Slow by Daniel Kahneman
  • The Gene: An Intimate History by Siddhartha Mukherjee
  • The Rise and Fall of Dinosaurs by Steve Brusatte
  • Prisoner's Dilemma: John Von Neumann, Game Theory, and the Puzzle of the Bomb by William Poundstone
  • The Black Swan: The Impact of the Highly Improbable by Nassim Nicholas Taleb
  • The Brothers Karamazov by Fyodor Dostoevsky
  • The Mahabharata translated by Bibek Debroy
  • Chomana Dudi by Shivarama Karanth
  • Sandhyaraga by A N Krishna Rao
